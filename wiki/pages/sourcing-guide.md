# Sourcing Guide

Where and how to source spare parts in Indonesia.

## Sourcing Channels

### 1. Authorized Distributors

**Best for**: OEM parts, warranty, consistent supply

| Supplier | Location | MOQ | Lead Time |
|----------|----------|-----|-----------|
| Hino Motors Sales | Jakarta | 10 pcs | 7-14 days |
| Isuzu Astra | Jakarta/Bandung | 10 pcs | 7-14 days |
| Mitsubishi Motors Krama Yudha | Jakarta | 10 pcs | 14-21 days |

**Pros**: Original guarantee, technical support
**Cons**: Higher price, MOQ requirements

### 2. Importers (Direct from Japan/Singapore)

**Best for**: Competitive OEM pricing, niche items

- **Bandung**: Multiple importers on Jl. Jendral Sudirman area
- **Surabaya**: Tanjung Perak port importers
- **Jakarta**: Pluit/Kelapa Gading area

**Typical Terms**:
- MOQ: 50-100 mixed items
- Price: 15-25% below distributor
- Payment: T/T 30% DP, 70% before shipping

### 3. Local Wholesalers

**Best for**: Quick stock, no MOQ

| Market | Focus | Payment Terms |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| Cipaganti (Bandung) | Mixed brands | Cash |
| Riau (Bandung) | Electrical | Cash/7 days |
| Tanah Abang | Budget parts | Cash |

**Pros**: Immediate availability, no MOQ, can inspect
**Cons**: Quality varies, higher per-unit price

### 4. Online Marketplaces (Reverse)

**Best for**: Clearing stock, competitor liquidation

- Buy from closing shops
- Stock clearance auctions
- Bundle deals from other sellers

## Risk Management

### Red Flags
- Prices too good to be true
- No warranty offered
- Supplier can't explain origin
- Asking cash only, no invoice
- Pushy sales tactics

### Verification Steps
1. Check packaging quality
2. Compare with known authentic
3. Test on own vehicle first
4. Get warranty in writing
5. Start with small order
