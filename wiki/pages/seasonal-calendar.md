# Seasonal Calendar

Commercial vehicle spare parts demand follows truck/bus operational cycles.

## Annual Cycle

### Q1 (January–March): Post-Holiday Slump
- **Demand**: Low
- **Cash flow**: Tight (customers recovering from holiday spending)
- **Strategy**: Clearance sales on slow-moving stock
- **Action**: Reduce inventory, focus on collections

### Q2 (April–June): Ramadan & Lebaran Rush
- **Demand**: Very High
- **Peak period**: 2 weeks before Lebaran
- **Why**: Logistics companies maximize deliveries before holidays
- **Strategy**: Stock up 6 weeks in advance
- **Action**: Pre-orders, bulk discounts for fleets

### Q3 (July–September): Recovery & Planting Season
- **Demand**: Moderate
- **Agricultural areas**: Slight uptick (harvest transport)
- **Strategy**: Normal operations, rebuild inventory
- **Action**: Scout new suppliers, test new SKUs

### Q4 (October–December): Year-End Peak
- **Demand**: Highest
- **Why**: Budget spending, pre-price-increase stocking
- **Period**: November–December peak
- **Strategy**: Maximize inventory, full margin pricing
- **Action**: Year-end promotions, B2B contract renewals

## Seasonal Pricing

| Period | Pricing Strategy |
|--------|------------------|
| Peak (Apr–May, Nov–Dec) | Full margin, minimal discounts |
| Shoulder (Mar, Sep–Oct) | Moderate discounts for volume |
| Slow (Jan–Feb, Jun–Aug) | Aggressive clearance |
