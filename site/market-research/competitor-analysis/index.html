    PART D: KEY LESSONS FOR YOUR SHOP¶

    For Consumer Car Parts¶

    1. One-Stop Shop Strategy (Jasutra Model)
    2. Carry tires, filters, oil, bulbs, wipers, car care
    3. Customers buy multiple items per transaction

    4. Higher average order value (AOV)

    5. Niche Dominance Strategy (Bohlamhid Model)

    6. Own ONE category (spark plugs, filters, etc.)
    7. Build brand authority

    8. Offer reseller program for B2B growth

    9. OEM Authority Strategy (DENSO Model)

    10. Partner with OEM brands (DENSO, NGK, T-OPT)
    11. Highlight manufacturer relationships

    12. Target workshops needing genuine parts for warranty repairs

    13. Multi-Platform Presence

    14. All top sellers are on Tokopedia + Shopee + TikTok
    15. Different platforms = different customer segments

    16. TikTok Shop = younger buyers, Tokopedia = older/professional

    17. Vehicle Compatibility in Titles

    18. Include car models in product titles
    19. Example: "Busi NGK Innova Fortuner" not just "Busi NGK"
    20. Improves search visibility

    For Commercial Vehicle Parts¶

    1. Technical Accuracy is Non-Negotiable
    2. Display exact OEM part numbers
    3. Include engine codes (4HK1, J08E, J08C)

    4. Fleet managers search by part number, not description

    5. EURO 4/5 Specialization = Higher Margin

    6. Modern trucks require specialized parts
    7. Lower competition due to complexity

    8. Higher margins (Rp1.9M - Rp6.25M per item)

    9. Offer OEM + Aftermarket Options

    10. Budget customers: Sakura, Hengst, aftermarket
    11. Premium customers: WABCO, BOSCH, genuine parts

    12. Capture both market segments

    13. Warranty = Trust

    14. Commercial buyers need assurance for expensive parts
    15. Offer warranty for OEM parts

    16. "Garansi Original" in product titles

    17. Multi-Brand Coverage

    18. Don't limit to one truck brand
    19. Cover HINO + Isuzu + Mitsubishi + Mercedes
    20. Fleet operators have mixed fleets

    Pricing Recommendations¶

    Category Recommended Strategy
    Consumer - Recurring Items (filters, spark plugs) Budget to mid-range - compete on price
    Consumer - Premium Items (tires, batteries) Mid-range - balance price vs. trust
    Consumer - Accessories (car mats, bulbs) Budget - impulse purchases
    Commercial - Filters Both OEM + aftermarket options
    Commercial - EURO 4 Parts Premium - specialized, low competition
    Commercial - Major Components (turbo, injectors) Premium with warranty
