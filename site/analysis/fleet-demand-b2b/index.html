    SECTION 2: B2B SELLING OPPORTUNITIES¶

    2.1 Tokopedia B2B Platform¶

    What is Tokopedia B2B?¶

    Current Status: Tokopedia's dedicated B2B platform (tokopedia.com/b2b) appears to be inactive or redirected. The URL shows a placeholder seller page.

    Alternative B2B Features on Tokopedia:

    Feature Status How to Access
    Tokopedia B2B (tokopedia.com/b2b) Inactive/Redirected Not currently functional
    Tokopedia Enterprise Active but limited Contact-based, for corporate buyers
    Tokopedia untuk Bisnis Integrated into Seller Center Available to all sellers
    Grosir/Bulk Listings Active Use "Grosir" keyword in listings
    COD for B2B Available Enable for bulk orders

    Key Finding: Tokopedia does NOT have a dedicated B2B marketplace. B2B sales happen through: 1. Regular listings marked as "Grosir" (wholesale) 2. Direct negotiation via chat 3. Custom quotation requests 4. Offline contracts (after initial contact via Tokopedia)

    How to Sell B2B on Tokopedia (Workaround)¶

    Step-by-Step Guide:

    1. Create Wholesale Listings
    2. Add "Grosir" in product title (e.g., "Grosir Filter Oli Hino 10 pcs")
    3. Set wholesale pricing tiers in description

    4. Include minimum order quantity (MOQ)

    5. Enable Bulk Pricing 

      Price Example:
- 1-4 pcs: Rp 150,000/unit (retail)
- 5-9 pcs: Rp 135,000/unit (10% discount)
- 10+ pcs: Rp 120,000/unit (20% discount)

    6. Target Business Buyers

    7. Use keywords: "grosir", "partai", "wholesale", "reseller"
    8. Create separate listing for bulk orders

    9. Offer free shipping for large orders

    10. Negotiate Offline

    11. Use Tokopedia chat for initial contact
    12. Exchange business contacts for contract
    13. Process large orders via manual invoice

    Contact Method: - Email: enterprise@tokopedia.com - Through Tokopedia official channels

    Realistic Assessment: This program is primarily for LARGE buyers (hundreds of millions IDR annually). Small sellers cannot directly access enterprise features.

    2.6 Payment Terms for Fleet Operators¶

    Standard Payment Terms:

    Term Type Description When Used Risk Level
    COD (Cash on Delivery) Payment on receipt New customers, small orders LOW
    Net 7 Payment within 7 days Established customers, small fleets LOW
    Net 14 Payment within 14 days Medium fleets, regular customers MEDIUM

    3.1 How Fleet Operators Decide Which Parts to Buy¶

    Decision-Making Hierarchy:

    Role Influence Decision Area
    Fleet Manager HIGH Overall budget, supplier selection, contract approval
    Procurement Officer HIGH Vendor negotiation, price comparison, order placement
    Workshop Supervisor MEDIUM Technical specifications, brand preference, quality check
    Mechanic LOW-MEDIUM Daily parts requests, urgent needs, brand familiarity

    Decision Factors (Ranked):

    Factor Weight Notes
    Price 30% Always important, but not top factor
    Quality/Reliability 25% Critical for safety parts (brakes, suspension)
    Availability/Lead Time 20% Vehicle downtime costs money
    Brand Trust 15% OEM vs aftermarket preference
    Payment Terms 10% Important for cash flow management

    Preference by Fleet Type:

    Fleet Type OEM Preference Aftermarket Preference Notes

    Mining Fleet 70-80% 20-30% Harsh conditions, reliability critical
    Plantation Fleet 30-40% 60-70% Cost-conscious, remote locations

    Small Trucking (Owner-operator) 20-30% 70-80% Cost is primary factor

    Key Insight: - Government fleets = ALMOST ALL OEM - Private fleets = MIXED (trend toward aftermarket for cost) - E-commerce boom = HIGH OPPORTUNITY for aftermarket parts

    SECTION 4: CONTRACT & TENDER OPPORTUNITIES¶

    4.1 Government Fleet Procurement Process¶

    Government Procurement System (LKPP)¶

    The National Public Procurement Agency (LKPP) oversees all government procurement.

    Key Portal: SIRUP (Sistem Informasi Rencana Umum Pengadaan) - https://sirup.lkpp.go.id (main portal) - Shows all planned government procurements

    Procurement Methods:

    Method Threshold Process
    Direct Appointment Below Rp 200 million Direct negotiation
    Quick Tender Rp 200 million - Rp 2 billion Faster process, limited bidders
    Open Tender Above Rp 2 billion Full public bidding
    E-Tendering All above threshold Electronic bidding via LPSE

    How to Participate in Government Tenders¶

    Step 1: Register as Vendor

    1. Visit the e-procurement portal:
    2. TransJakarta: https://eproc.transjakarta.co.id
    3. DAMRI: https://eproc.damri.co.id
    4. LPSE Jakarta: https://lpse.jakarta.go.id

    5. LPSE National: https://inaproc.lkpp.go.id

    6. Register vendor account (requires):

    7. NPWP (Tax ID)
    8. NIB (Business Identification Number)
    9. SIUP/NIB (Business License)
    10. Company deed (Akta Perusahaan)
    11. Bank account in company name

    12. Tax clearance (SPPKP)

    13. Complete vendor qualification:

    14. Small business (Usaha Kecil)
    15. Non-small business (Non-UK)
    16. Able to bid on appropriate categories

    Step 2: Find Relevant Tenders

    1. Monitor tender announcements:
    2. Check SIRUP for planned procurements
    3. Subscribe to tender notifications

    4. Join industry associations for leads

    5. Download tender documents:

    6. Free download from portal
    7. Review specifications
    8. Check eligibility requirements

    Step 3: Prepare Bid

    Required documents typically include: - Technical proposal (product specifications) - Price proposal (HPS - Harga Perkiraan Sendiri) - Company profile & financial statements - Track record/references - Product samples (for evaluation) - Certifications (if required)

    Step 4: Submit Bid

    • Upload to e-procurement system
    • Submit before deadline
    • Pay bid security (jasa penawaran) - typically 1-3% of bid value

    Step 5: Auction & Evaluation

    • Price auction (if multiple qualified bidders)
    • Technical evaluation
    • Price comparison
    • Winner announcement

    Recent DAMRI Tender (from eproc.damri.co.id):

    4.3 Certifications Required for Government Contracts¶

    Minimum Requirements:

    Requirement Description Where to Obtain
    NIB (Nomor Induk Berusaha) Business registration OSS (Online Single Submission)
    NPWP Perusahaan Company tax ID Tax office (KPP)
    SIUP/NIB Business license OSS system
    TDP Company registration Not needed if have NIB
    SKT (Sertifikat Keterangan Terdaftar) Vendor registration LPSE portal
    SPPKP Taxable entrepreneur status Tax office

    Additional for Larger Contracts:

    Requirement Description Notes
    ISO 9001 Quality management For contracts above Rp 1 billion
    ISO 14001 Environmental management For certain categories
    TKDN (Tingkat Komponen Dalam Negeri) Local content requirement Minimum 40% for certain products
    Product Certification (SNI) Indonesian National Standard For safety-critical parts
    Authorized Distributor Letter From brand principal For OEM parts

    4.4 Tender Strategy for Spare Parts Sellers¶

    Tier 1: Start Small (Rp 50-200 million contracts)

    • Target: Regional government fleets, city transport
    • Focus: Filters, brake parts, consumables
    • Competition: Lower
    • Win probability: Higher

    Tier 2: Medium Contracts (Rp 200 million - Rp 1 billion)

    • Target: Provincial fleets, DAMRI regional
    • Focus: Complete maintenance packages
    • Requirements: Track record, references
    • Competition: Medium

    Tier 3: Large Contracts (Rp 1+ billion)

    • Target: TransJakarta, DAMRI national, BUMN
    • Focus: Long-term supply contracts (OSPS)
    • Requirements: ISO certification, large capital
    • Competition: High (limited to established players)

    Practical Approach: 1. Start with direct sales to fleet workshops 2. Build track record (2-3 years) 3. Register on LPSE portals 4. Bid on small tenders first 5. Build relationships with procurement officers 6. Scale up to larger contracts

    5.3 Can You Supply to Authorized Service Centers?¶

    Official Answer: NO

    Authorized service centers have exclusive supply agreements with brand principals. They cannot legally buy from independent suppliers.

    Exceptions (Limited): - Emergency parts when principal is out of stock - Non-warranty repairs on older vehicles - Used/older vehicles no longer supported by principal

    7.3 Key Success Factors for B2B Sales¶

    1. Price Competitiveness - Offer 15-25% below retail for bulk orders - Match wholesale market prices - Consider volume-based pricing tiers

    2. Delivery Speed - Same-day delivery for Jakarta area - 1-2 days for Java, Sumatra - Use multiple logistics partners (truck, train, cargo)

    3. Product Quality - Stand behind products with warranty - Replace defective parts quickly - Build reputation for reliability

    4. Payment Flexibility - Offer credit terms for established customers - Accept multiple payment methods - Consider installment for large orders

    5. Technical Support - Provide product specifications - Offer installation guidance - Help with parts identification

    6. Relationship Building - Regular customer visits - WhatsApp group for updates - Holiday greetings and appreciation
