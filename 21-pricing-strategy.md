# COMPREHENSIVE PRICING STRATEGY
## Online Selling Pricing Framework for Commercial Vehicle Spare Parts | April 2026

**Research Date:** April 9, 2026  
**Business:** Luxor Parts (Bandung)  
**Core Philosophy:** *"Same offline price online. Let the market expand, not the price."*

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The pricing strategy for selling commercial vehicle spare parts online is built on a simple principle: **maintain the same prices offline and online**. Online selling adds approximately **5-7%** in platform commissions and payment fees, plus **Rp 3,000 - 8,000** per order in subsidized shipping costs. However, this investment unlocks access to the **entire Indonesian archipelago** — all 34 provinces, 17,000+ islands — instead of limiting sales to Bandung walk-in customers and occasional phone orders from nearby cities.

**The Math is Simple:**
- Before: 30 years serving Bandung + occasional West Java
- After: Reach fleet operators in Surabaya, Medan, Makassar, Balikpapan, Papua
- Additional cost: ~7% platform fees + subsidized shipping
- Additional reach: **100x more potential customers** (from ~50k to ~50M commercial vehicle operators)

**Key Insight:** The offline store doesn't lose customers — it gains an additional channel. Urgent needs still require immediate pickup, but scheduled maintenance and advance ordering now happen online. The pie gets bigger; it doesn't just shift slices.

### 1.1 The "Price per kg" Rule

**Golden Rule:** Only sell products online where **Selling Price ÷ Weight > Rp 75,000/kg**

This rule ensures shipping costs don't eat margins. Products that fail this test should remain offline-only.

|--------------|--------------|---------------------|
| **Parts under Rp 30,000** | Platform fees (Rp 5,000+) eat all margin | Offline only |
| **Parts > 20kg** | Shipping cost prohibitive | Phone order + pickup or freight |
| **Requires technical fitting** | Customer blames seller for wrong fit | Offline only (with mechanic) |
| **Hazardous materials** | Shipping restrictions | Offline pickup only |
| **Very fragile items** | Breakage risk in shipping | Offline with packaging |
| **Special order/custom** | Lead time issues | Phone order only |

## 2. PRICING TIERS

### 2.1 Complete Pricing Structure

| Customer Type | Product Price | Shipping | Condition | Benefits |
|---------------|---------------|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Online retail (1-3 items)** | Same as offline | Customer pays full | Standard order | Reach new cities |
| **Online retail (4+ items)** | Same as offline | **50% off shipping** | Minimum 4 SKUs | Encourage bundling |
| **Online retail (Rp 500k+ Java)** | Same as offline | **FREE shipping** | Java only | Key threshold |
| **Online retail (Rp 1M+ Luar Jawa)** | Same as offline | **FREE shipping** | Outside Java | Big orders anywhere |
| **Paket Servis Berkala** | **-5% from offline** | FREE (Java) | Pre-built kits | Fleet customers |
| **Offline walk-in** | Offline standard | N/A | Physical visit | Immediate availability |
| **Offline repeat customer** | Current discount | N/A | Existing system | Unchanged |
| **Workshop/Pak Tani** | Current wholesale | N/A | Existing system | Unchanged |

### 2.2 Bundle Pricing Logic

**Free Shipping Threshold Rationale:**
- **Rp 500,000 for Java:** Covers 70% of typical fleet service orders
- **Rp 1,000,000 for Outside Java:** Compensates for higher inter-island shipping costs
- **Bundle 4+ items:** Increases average order value from Rp 125k to Rp 400k+

**Shipping Subsidy Calculation:**
| Destination | Order Value | Actual Shipping | Seller Subsidy | Platform Subsidy | Customer Pays |
|-------------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------|---------------|

## 6. THE "MAINTENANCE GUY" INSIGHT

- Age: 35-55 years old
- Role: Workshop manager / Fleet supervisor at logistics, mining, or distribution company
- Vehicles: 10-50 trucks, buses, or commercial vehicles
- Pain Point: Keeping all vehicles operational with minimal downtime

**Current Buying Behavior:**
- Visits Luxor Bandung every 1-3 months
- Spends Rp 5-15 million per visit
- Buys predictable consumables: filters, brake pads, belts, seals

**Their Problem (Before Online):**
- Must travel to Bandung (time away from workshop)

- No digital records for their inventory system

**Online Solution:**
- Paket Servis: One click for complete service needs
- Scheduled delivery: Order Tuesday, receive Thursday for Friday service

- Cross-reference: Upload photo of old part, we confirm compatibility

### Reality: Channels serve different needs

| **Urgent breakdown** | ✅ Walk-in, immediate pickup | ❌ 2-3 days too slow |
| **Scheduled maintenance** | ❌ Must plan trip to Bandung | ✅ Order ahead, delivered Thursday for Friday service |
| **Technical advice needed** | ✅ Talk to Pak Tani in person | ❌ Chat only, limited diagnostic |
| **Heavy/bulk items** | ✅ Can inspect before taking |❌ Risk of damage in shipping |
| **Out-of-town customers** | ❌ Impossible to visit | ✅ Only option |
| **Price comparison** | ❌ Must ask in person | ✅ Easy browsing |

## 9. PRICING STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS

### Immediate Actions (Month 1)

1. **Start with 100-150 SKUs** — products with best price/kg ratio (>Rp 75,000/kg)
2. **Same offline prices for everything** — no discount for being online
3. **Free shipping on Rp 500k+ (Java)** — converts 2x better than paid shipping
4. **Create 5 Paket Servis** — start with Isuzu NMR71, Mitsubishi Colt, Hino FM320
5. **Tokopedia first** — B2B trust is critical; TikTok Shop can wait 3 months

### Month 2-3 Actions

6. **Encourage bank transfer over COD** — saves Rp 5,000-10,000 per transaction in fees
7. **Target fleet maintenance managers** — reach out to existing offline customers in other cities
8. **Don't list parts under Rp 30k** — platform fees eat the margin
9. **Don't list items over 20kg** — unless pre-arranged freight
10. **Track best-selling products** — expand catalog based on data, not guesses

### Month 6+ Actions

11. **When hitting 500+ orders/month** → upgrade to Tokopedia Power Shop (saves 2% commission)
12. **Launch TikTok Shop** — after Tokopedia is stable, use TikTok for consumables/filters with video demos
13. **Expand Paket Servis** — build 10 packages covering all popular local vehicles

### What NOT to Do

| ❌ Don't | ✅ Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|
| Discount online prices vs offline | Same price,但 reach is the advantage |
| Race to lowest price | Premium positioning, justify with support |
| Accept COD for all orders | Encourage transfer with Rp 5k discount |
| List everything online | Filter by price/kg and urgency first |
| Ignore returns | Fast, friendly returns build long-term trust |

## CONCLUSION

The pricing strategy is simple but powerful:

1. **Same price online and offline** — expansion, not discount
2. **Free shipping above thresholds** — converts customers, minimal seller cost
3. **Paket Servis for fleets** — loyalty + efficiency = better margins
4. **Focus on maintenance guy** — the customer who makes this business work
5. **Don't fear cannibalization** — channels complement, don't compete

**Bottom Line:** Online selling costs ~7% more per transaction, but reaches 100x more customers. For commercial vehicle spare parts with 30-35% margins, this is a profitable expansion.
