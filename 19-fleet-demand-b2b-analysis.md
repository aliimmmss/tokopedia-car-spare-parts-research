## SECTION 2: B2B SELLING OPPORTUNITIES

### 2.1 Tokopedia B2B Platform

#### What is Tokopedia B2B?

**Current Status:** Tokopedia's dedicated B2B platform (tokopedia.com/b2b) appears to be **inactive or redirected**. The URL shows a placeholder seller page.

**Alternative B2B Features on Tokopedia:**

| Feature | Status | How to Access |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| **Tokopedia B2B (tokopedia.com/b2b)** | Inactive/Redirected | Not currently functional |
| **Tokopedia Enterprise** | Active but limited | Contact-based, for corporate buyers |
| **Tokopedia untuk Bisnis** | Integrated into Seller Center | Available to all sellers |
| **Grosir/Bulk Listings** | Active | Use "Grosir" keyword in listings |
| **COD for B2B** | Available | Enable for bulk orders |

**Key Finding:** Tokopedia does NOT have a dedicated B2B marketplace. B2B sales happen through:
1. Regular listings marked as "Grosir" (wholesale)
2. Direct negotiation via chat
3. Custom quotation requests
4. Offline contracts (after initial contact via Tokopedia)

---

#### How to Sell B2B on Tokopedia (Workaround)

**Step-by-Step Guide:**

1. **Create Wholesale Listings**
   - Add "Grosir" in product title (e.g., "Grosir Filter Oli Hino 10 pcs")
   - Set wholesale pricing tiers in description
   - Include minimum order quantity (MOQ)

2. **Enable Bulk Pricing**
   ```
   Price Example:
   - 1-4 pcs: Rp 150,000/unit (retail)
   - 5-9 pcs: Rp 135,000/unit (10% discount)
   - 10+ pcs: Rp 120,000/unit (20% discount)
   ```

3. **Target Business Buyers**
   - Use keywords: "grosir", "partai", "wholesale", "reseller"
   - Create separate listing for bulk orders
   - Offer free shipping for large orders

4. **Negotiate Offline**
   - Use Tokopedia chat for initial contact
   - Exchange business contacts for contract
   - Process large orders via manual invoice

### 3.1 How Fleet Operators Decide Which Parts to Buy

**Decision-Making Hierarchy:**

| Role | Influence | Decision Area |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| **Fleet Manager** | HIGH | Overall budget, supplier selection, contract approval |
| **Procurement Officer** | HIGH | Vendor negotiation, price comparison, order placement |
| **Workshop Supervisor** | MEDIUM | Technical specifications, brand preference, quality check |
| **Mechanic** | LOW-MEDIUM | Daily parts requests, urgent needs, brand familiarity |

**Decision Factors (Ranked):**

| Factor | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Price** | 30% | Always important, but not top factor |
| **Quality/Reliability** | 25% | Critical for safety parts (brakes, suspension) |
| **Availability/Lead Time** | 20% | Vehicle downtime costs money |
| **Brand Trust** | 15% | OEM vs aftermarket preference |
| **Payment Terms** | 10% | Important for cash flow management |

## SECTION 4: CONTRACT & TENDER OPPORTUNITIES

### 4.1 Government Fleet Procurement Process

#### Government Procurement System (LKPP)

**The National Public Procurement Agency (LKPP) oversees all government procurement.**

**Key Portal:** SIRUP (Sistem Informasi Rencana Umum Pengadaan)
- https://sirup.lkpp.go.id (main portal)
- Shows all planned government procurements

**Procurement Methods:**

| Method | Threshold | Process |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **Direct Appointment** | Below Rp 200 million | Direct negotiation |
| **Quick Tender** | Rp 200 million - Rp 2 billion | Faster process, limited bidders |
| **Open Tender** | Above Rp 2 billion | Full public bidding |
| **E-Tendering** | All above threshold | Electronic bidding via LPSE |

---

#### How to Participate in Government Tenders

**Step 1: Register as Vendor**

1. Visit the e-procurement portal:
   - **TransJakarta:** https://eproc.transjakarta.co.id
   - **DAMRI:** https://eproc.damri.co.id
   - **LPSE Jakarta:** https://lpse.jakarta.go.id
   - **LPSE National:** https://inaproc.lkpp.go.id

2. Register vendor account (requires):
   - NPWP (Tax ID)
   - NIB (Business Identification Number)
   - SIUP/NIB (Business License)
   - Company deed (Akta Perusahaan)
   - Bank account in company name
   - Tax clearance (SPPKP)

3. Complete vendor qualification:
   - Small business (Usaha Kecil)
   - Non-small business (Non-UK)
   - Able to bid on appropriate categories

**Step 2: Find Relevant Tenders**

1. Monitor tender announcements:
   - Check SIRUP for planned procurements
   - Subscribe to tender notifications
   - Join industry associations for leads

2. Download tender documents:
   - Free download from portal
   - Review specifications
   - Check eligibility requirements

**Step 3: Prepare Bid**

Required documents typically include:
- Technical proposal (product specifications)
- Price proposal (HPS - Harga Perkiraan Sendiri)
- Company profile & financial statements
- Track record/references
- Product samples (for evaluation)
- Certifications (if required)

**Step 4: Submit Bid**

- Upload to e-procurement system
- Submit before deadline
- Pay bid security (jasa penawaran) - typically 1-3% of bid value

**Step 5: Auction & Evaluation**

- Price auction (if multiple qualified bidders)
- Technical evaluation
- Price comparison
- Winner announcement

### 4.4 Tender Strategy for Spare Parts Sellers

**Tier 1: Start Small (Rp 50-200 million contracts)**

- Target: Regional government fleets, city transport
- Focus: Filters, brake parts, consumables
- Competition: Lower
- Win probability: Higher

**Tier 2: Medium Contracts (Rp 200 million - Rp 1 billion)**

- Target: Provincial fleets, DAMRI regional
- Focus: Complete maintenance packages
- Requirements: Track record, references
- Competition: Medium

**Tier 3: Large Contracts (Rp 1+ billion)**

- Target: TransJakarta, DAMRI national, BUMN
- Focus: Long-term supply contracts (OSPS)
- Requirements: ISO certification, large capital
- Competition: High (limited to established players)

**Practical Approach:**
1. Start with direct sales to fleet workshops
2. Build track record (2-3 years)
3. Register on LPSE portals
4. Bid on small tenders first
5. Build relationships with procurement officers
6. Scale up to larger contracts

### 5.3 Can You Supply to Authorized Service Centers?

**Official Answer: NO**

Authorized service centers have exclusive supply agreements with brand principals. They cannot legally buy from independent suppliers.

**Exceptions (Limited):**
- Emergency parts when principal is out of stock
- Non-warranty repairs on older vehicles
- Used/older vehicles no longer supported by principal

**Realistic Opportunity:**

| Scenario | Opportunity Level | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| **OEM parts supply** | ZERO | Must be authorized distributor |
| **Aftermarket to authorized centers** | ALMOST ZERO | Contractually forbidden |
| **Supply to independent workshops** | HIGH | Primary market for aftermarket |
| **Supply to fleet in-house workshops** | VERY HIGH | Major opportunity |
